# Meules — Catalog

Claude Monet's *Meules* (Wheatstacks / Grainstacks) series, as currently
held in this folder. Titles are the canonical French titles; English
time-of-year and time-of-day follow in parentheses at the end of each
filename so the rotation bucket is readable at a glance.

Years and Wildenstein numbers are the standard attributions; where
uncertain, marked with `?`. This catalog is the source-of-truth for
the desktop rotation spec in
`wheatstacks-rice/ideas/wheatstacks-rotation.md`.

## Naming convention

```
<French title>, <year> (Season, Time-of-day).jpg
```

- **Meule** = singular = one stack. **Meules** = plural = two stacks.
  This is load-bearing where the same title exists in both versions
  (e.g. *effet de neige, le matin* in entries #5 and #13).
- Museum disambiguation in parens when two paintings share a title
  (e.g. the two *fin de l'été* works, entries #14 and #15).
- Season ∈ {Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter}.
- Time-of-day ∈ {Morning, Midday, Evening}. No Night; see gaps.

## Gaps against the rotation grid

Four genuine gaps remain out of the 12-bucket grid (plus night):

1. **`autumn.midday`** — nothing in the folder reads as clearly
   autumn midday. *Meules, fin du jour, automne* is evening, and the
   Giverny morning effect is morning. Closest substitute: use the
   *fin du jour* painting across both afternoon buckets, or alias
   autumn.midday to summer.midday.

2. **`spring.morning`** — Monet's series is heavy on summer, fall,
   and winter; spring-specific mornings are thin. *Meules, effet de
   gelée blanche* (1889 hoarfrost) could be pressed into service
   here as "cold spring morning" in place of winter.morning if a
   different winter morning is used.

3. **`spring.midday`** — no fit at all. Closest: *Meule, effet de
   dégel* (thaw), but that's evening.

4. **`any.night`** — Monet did not paint the *Meules* after dark;
   no true night painting exists. Options: (a) fall through to the
   most recent evening painting, (b) generate a luminance-dimmed
   copy of an existing evening, (c) omit.

Summer evening is over-stocked (five paintings); spring and autumn
are structurally under-stocked because Monet worked the series most
intensively across summer into winter 1890-91. If you ever want
parity per-bucket, the intra-bucket cycling mentioned in the spec's
open question is where that happens.

## Changes since last revision

- Added *Meules, fin de l'été (AIC)* (W.1269) as a distinct work
  from the existing Musée d'Orsay version. The two were previously
  conflated in this catalog.
- Corrected file naming to include an English (Season, Time-of-day)
  suffix on every entry.
- Removed two lower-resolution duplicates that came in with this
  batch: *Monet - Stack of Wheat (Thaw, Sunset)* and *Monet - Stacks
  of Wheat (Sunset, Snow Effect)*.
- Reclassified *Meules, effet de gelée blanche* (1889) from
  `spring.morning` to `winter.morning` — a hoarfrost scene is
  canonically a cold-morning effect, and the rose dawn sky supports
  that. This leaves `spring.morning` as a real gap rather than a
  stretched assignment.
